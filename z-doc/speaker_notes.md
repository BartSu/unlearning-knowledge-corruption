## p03 · Research problem (60 s)

**Opening**:
> "The problem is this: LLM unlearning is needed whenever copyright, privacy or toxic content has to be deleted from a trained model. But unlearning is **not surgical** — forgetting a set $\mathcal{D}_f$ simultaneously damages knowledge unrelated to $\mathcal{D}_f$. I call that **knowledge corruption**."

**Body**:
> "And it is expensive to measure: evaluating **one** forget set's three-layer corruption profile already needs one unlearn run plus cross-evaluation on many triplets. Sweeping 100 candidate forget sets to compare them becomes hundreds of GPU-hours — this does not scale."

**Close with the question on the slide**:
> "So: how can we cheaply and systematically assess the corruption a forget set will cause?"

**Cue**: **Stress the word "systematically"** — it flags the Act II motivation (audit, not one-off). Avoid getting pulled into "what is unlearning" basics; assume the audience knows.

## p04 · Experimental setup (60 s)
